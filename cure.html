Select Your PCOD Intensity Level

Diet Plan

Recommended Diet for Mild PCOD

Start your day with warm lemon water
Include high-fiber foods (oats, quinoa, brown rice)
Eat plenty of green leafy vegetables
Include lean proteins (fish, chicken, tofu)
Limit processed sugars and refined carbs
Maintain regular meal timings and stay hydrated throughout the day

Recommended Diet for Moderate PCOD

Follow a low-glycemic diet
Include anti-inflammatory foods
Increase protein intake (eggs, legumes, lean meats)
Add omega-3 rich foods (fatty fish, nuts)
Avoid dairy and gluten if sensitive
Consider intermittent fasting after consulting your healthcare provider

Recommended Diet for Severe PCOD

Strict low-carb, anti-inflammatory diet
Focus on whole, unprocessed foods
Include specific supplements (as prescribed)
Eliminate inflammatory foods
Regular meal timing with portion control
Work with a nutritionist for a personalized meal plan

Exercise Plan

Recommended Exercise for Mild PCOD

30 minutes of moderate cardio (5 days/week)
Light strength training (2-3 days/week)
Yoga or stretching exercises
Daily walking for 30 minutes
Light swimming or cycling
Focus on consistency rather than intensity

Recommended Exercise for Moderate PCOD

45 minutes of cardio (5 days/week)
Moderate strength training (3-4 days/week)
HIIT workouts (2 days/week)
Stress-reducing activities like yoga
Core strengthening exercises
Balance between cardio and strength training is crucial

Recommended Exercise for Severe PCOD

Structured exercise program with professional guidance
Low-impact cardio exercises
Gentle strength training with proper form
Therapeutic yoga or pilates
Regular stretching and mobility work
Start slowly and gradually increase intensity under supervision
